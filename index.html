Indus Valley Civilization

The Indus Valley Civilization (circa 3300–1300 BCE) was one of the world’s earliest urban cultures, known for its advanced city planning, architecture, and social organization. Major sites like Harappa and Mohenjo-Daro reveal the ingenuity of its people.

Vedic Period

Following the decline of the Indus Valley, the Vedic Period (circa 1500–500 BCE) marked the arrival of the Indo-Aryans. This era saw the composition of the Vedas, the foundational texts of Hinduism, and the establishment of various kingdoms.

Islamic Influence Regional Kingdoms From the 8th century onwards, India saw the rise of various Islamic empires, including the Delhi Sultanate (1206–1526) and the Mughal Empire (1526–1857). This era brought profound cultural and architectural changes, exemplified by the Taj Mahal. Regional Kingdoms Simultaneously, numerous regional kingdoms flourished, contributing to the diverse tapestry of Indian culture. The Vijayanagara Empire in the South and the Maratha Empire in the West are notable examples.
